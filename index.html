So, what's this all about then?

The basics

With multiplayer, co-op and singleplayer campaigns, players explore an absurd fantasy world in an attempt to prove themselves the best of the realm's reject Lords and Ladies.

You and up to 3 of your worst enemies, mothers-in-law (or even friends) can play.

Each player plans and
lodges their turn

at the same time

meaning play turns are more responsive

and the game is faster and more action packed - like Keanu Reeves in Speed.

Plus, you'll be able to choose from multiple play modes which change victory conditions or the style of play.

Single player vs. AI multiplayer games

Bone up on your skills or show the AI who's boss. We've given AI players the ability to feel the emotion and sting of losing a game, so don't go too hard on them.

And just how does one prove that one is the best?

The concept

You'll be able to win the game through force of might, clever crafting, solving the ails of the lands or by amassing the most wealth.

Or, you know, by being

conniving

and deceiving your unsuspecting, nice friends...

With multiple victory paths, you can play the way you like and tailor the game to your whims.

Maybe you could win by being the most

handsome?

...nah lol jokes, you can't win that way.

Metrognomes, Hyper-intelligent Wolves, Orcs on Unicycles and Hungarian, Hungarian Hippos are just some of the Perils that plague the land. Strength of arms is one path to victory - do you face the perils yourself, or turn them against your opponent?

Your resources, companions, equipment and events - all are represented by cards. But your hand size is limited.

Even the items you craft are cards. The game will automatically play some cards when you need them, so having a large hand can really benefit your game.

Tailor the game to your playstyle. You'll be able to win with a combination of:

Strength of arms

Craftsmastery

Solving quests

Exploration

or even

Backstabbery & deceit
